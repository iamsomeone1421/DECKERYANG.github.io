World War II

World War II, which was seen as the continuation of World War II (see World War I page for more info), lasted from September 1, 1939 to September 2, 1945. Over these 6 years of war, an estimated number of between 40 and 55 million lives were lost, making World War II the most deadly conflict in history. This war involved majority of the worlds nations divided into two opposing military alliances: The Allies and the Axis/ Central powers

What were the causes of World War II?

-The Treaty of Versailles left Germany and resentful, helping Hitler rise to power.

-The Great Depression caused global economic struggles, increasing support for extremist governments.

-Germany, Italy, and Japan pursued aggressive territorial expansion and militarism.

-The League of Nations failed to stop these actions, while appeasement by Britain and France encouraged further aggression.

-The invasion of Poland by Germany in 1939 directly triggered the outbreak of the War.

-Together, these factors created complex and economic tensions that led to World War II.

Timeline of World War II

Pre War developments

1919: Treaty of Versailles imposes harsh conditions on Germany, sowing seeds of resentment.

1933: Adolf Hitler becomes Chancellor of Germany, Nazi party rises to power

1938: Germany annexes Austria and later the Sudentland following the Munich Agreement.

August 1939: Nazi-Soviet Non-Aggression Pact is signed, secretly diving Eastern Europe between Germany and the Soviet Union.

1939: The War Begins

September 1, 1939: Germany invades Poland using blitzkrieg tactics, triggering the start of World War II

September 3, 1939: Britain, France, Australia, and New Zealand declare war on Germany.

September 17, 1939: Soviet Union invades eastern Poland.

1940: Axis Expansion

May - June 1940: Germany invades Belgium, the Netherlands, and France. France surrenders on June 22.

July - October 1940: Battle of Britain - The UK successfully defends against German air attacks.

September 27, 1940: Germany, Italy, and Japan sign the Tripartite Pact, forming the Axis Powers.

1941: Global Conflict Escalates

June 22, 1941: Germany launches Operation Barbarossa, invading the Soviet Union.

December 7, 1941: Japan attacks Pearl Harbor; the US enters the war.

December 11, 1941: Germany and Italy declare war on the United States.

1942 - 1943: Turning Points

January 20, 1942: Wannsee Conference formalizes the Holocaust's "Final Solution".

June 4-7, 1942: Battle of Midway marks a decisive US bavak victory
